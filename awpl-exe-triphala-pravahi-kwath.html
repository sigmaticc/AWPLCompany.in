Triphala

Triphala, meaning "three fruits," is a traditional Ayurvedic blend made from haritaki (chebulic myrobalan), bibhitaki (beleric myrobalan), and amla (Indian gooseberry). Rich in vitamin C, it is usually prepared from dried fruit powder in equal or varying ratios. Used for centuries as an Ayurvedic tonic (rasayana), Triphala is one of the most popular natural remedies in India. In China, a related form called sanlejiang has been made for over 1,200 years, first as a fruit wine and now also as a non-alcoholic herbal drink.
